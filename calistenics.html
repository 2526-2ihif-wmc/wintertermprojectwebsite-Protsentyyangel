The planche is one of the hardest moves in calisthenics that makes people stop and stare. If you’ve ever seen someone holding their body perfectly parallel to the ground, supported only by their hands, you know exactly what I’m talking about. It’s not just impressive—it’s inspiring. When I first got into calisthenics, the planche felt like this mythical skill only a select few could achieve. Watching videos of people holding the position with seemingly no effort made me both excited and skeptical. Could I ever do that? The answer turned out to be “yes,” but not without a lot of work, trial and error, and lessons learned along the way.

What is the Plance?

The planche is a static hold where your entire body hovers parallel to the ground, supported only by your hands. It’s like a plank, but way harder because your feet aren’t touching the ground. The name comes from the French word "plank," and it’s been a staple in gymnastics and calisthenics for decades.

It is a skill that demands respect. You’re asking your shoulders, wrists, and core to work together in ways they likely never have before. It’s not just about getting stronger; it’s about discovering what your body can do when you put in the effort. Let’s not sugarcoat it, though: learning the planche is hard. It will test your patience, challenge your consistency, and push your limits. But the beauty of calisthenics is that progress happens in layers. Every small win—whether it’s holding a tuck planche for a second longer or feeling your wrists adapt to the pressure—brings you one step closer to the ultimate goal. In this guide, I’m going to walk you through everything I wish I knew when I first started chasing the planche. From foundational exercises to the common mistakes I made (and how to avoid them), this is your blueprint for mastering one of calisthenics’ most iconic skills. Whether you’re here to impress your friends or just prove to yourself that you can do it, you’re in the right place.

The Benefits of Training for the Planche

Training for the planche isn’t just about looking cool (though, let’s be honest, it does look awesome). It’s about what you gain along the way. The process of building the strength and mobility required for the planche has a ripple effect across your entire fitness journey. Here’s what you can expect when you start working toward this iconic skill.

If you are completely new to calisthenics, start with our Level 1 program to build the foundational strength. You can do it at home with no equipment. And it's free.

Incredible Upper Body Strength

The planche demands serious strength, particularly in your shoulders, chest, and triceps. Think of it as a total upper-body workout disguised as a skill. The straight-arm strength you build while leaning into progressions like the planche lean or the tuck planche will make traditional exercises, like push-ups and dips, feel easier and more controlled. You’ll start noticing power in movements you never thought twice about before.

When I started training for the planche, my shoulders burned in ways I didn’t know were possible. I quickly realized that this kind of strength translated into everything else I did, from handstands to explosive push-ups.

Stronger Core Than You Ever Thought Possible

You can’t fake core engagement when you’re training for the planche. Your abs, obliques, and even deeper stabilizing muscles all have to work overtime to keep your body aligned and hovering above the ground. The result? A rock-solid core that doesn’t just look good but actually supports you in everything you do—whether it’s lifting, running, or nailing other calisthenics moves like the front lever.
