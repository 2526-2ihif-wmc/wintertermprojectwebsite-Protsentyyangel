Types of Running

Different running workouts serve different purposes. Mix them throughout the week for balanced development.

üîÑ Easy Run

Pace: Conversational pace where you can easily talk

Purpose: Build aerobic base and recover

Frequency: 3-4 times per week

Duration: 30-60 minutes

‚ö° Tempo Run

Pace: Comfortably hard, can speak short sentences

Purpose: Improve lactate threshold and speed

Frequency: 1 time per week

Duration: 20-40 minutes at tempo pace

üöÄ Interval Training

Pace: Alternate between fast and slow

Purpose: Increase speed and VO2 max

Frequency: 1 time per week

Duration: Example: 5x (3min fast, 2min slow)

üèÉ Long Run

Pace: Easy, same as easy runs

Purpose: Build endurance and mental toughness

Frequency: 1 time per week

Duration: 60+ minutes, gradually increase

Proper Running Form

Proper form prevents injuries and makes running more efficient. Focus on these key points:

‚úì
Posture

Body upright with slight forward lean from ankles, not waist

‚úì
Head Position

Look ahead 10-15 meters, not down at feet

‚úì
Shoulders

Relaxed and down, not tense or raised

‚úì
Arms

Bent at 90 degrees, swing naturally front to back

‚úì
Feet

Land on midfoot, not heels. Quick light steps

‚úì
Cadence

170-180 steps per minute for optimal efficiency

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Running Too Fast

Most beginners run easy runs too fast. Stay conversational pace.

‚ùå Long Strides

Overstriding causes injury. Keep steps quick and light.

‚ùå No Warm-up

Always walk or jog slowly for 5-10 minutes first.

‚ùå Wrong Shoes

Get properly fitted running shoes for your gait.

‚ùå Too Much Too Soon

Don't increase distance more than 10% per week.

‚ùå Ignoring Pain

Sharp pain is a signal to stop. Rest and recover.

Nutrition for Runners

Proper nutrition is crucial for running performance and recovery.

ü•ó Pre-Run Nutrition (30-60 min before)

  • Short runs: Light snack - banana, granola bar
  • Long runs: Carbs + protein - oatmeal with banana
  • Hydration: Water 2-3 hours and 15 min before

ü•§ During-Run (for runs over 90 min)

  • Carbs: 30-60g per hour
  • Electrolytes: Sodium for hydration
  • Hydration: 400-800ml water per hour

üçå Post-Run Recovery (within 30 min)

  • 3-4:1 ratio: Carbs to protein
  • Examples: Chocolate milk, smoothie
  • Timing: Critical within 30-60 minutes

‚öñÔ∏è Daily Nutrition

  • Carbs: 5-7g per kg for moderate training
  • Protein: 1.4-1.6g per kg daily
  • Fats: 20-25% of total calories

Common Running Injuries & Prevention

Running injuries are preventable with proper training and recovery strategies.

Prevention Strategies

1

Progressive Overload: Increase weekly mileage by no more than 10%

2

Strength Training: 2-3x per week focusing on glutes, hamstrings, core

3

Flexibility Work: Dynamic stretching before, static after (10-15 min)

4

Proper Footwear: Get gait analyzed and choose appropriate shoes

5

Adequate Recovery: Include easy runs, rest days, 7-9 hours sleep

6

Warm-up & Cool-down: 5-10 minutes each for every run
