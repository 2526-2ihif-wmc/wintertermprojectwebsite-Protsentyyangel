Planche

The planche is one of the most impressive calisthenics skills where you hold your body parallel to the ground with only your hands touching the floor. This isometric hold requires extraordinary shoulder strength, core control, and body awareness. The planche is often considered one of the ultimate tests of bodyweight strength.

Why Train the Planche?

  • Builds exceptional shoulder strength and stability
  • Develops core control and coordination
  • Improves pushing power for other exercises
  • Incredible visual achievement and body control
  • Transfers strength to push-ups and handstands

Prerequisites

  • Strong push-up ability (30+ reps)
  • Good wrist flexibility and strength
  • Strong core and shoulders
  • Ability to hold a 60-second plank
  • Previous experience with advanced movements

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Progressing too quickly - This leads to wrist and shoulder injuries
  • Neglecting wrist preparation - Wrist strength is essential
  • Poor form early on - Focus on proper body alignment from the start
  • Skipping prerequisite exercises - Build the foundation first

How to Achieve Planche

  • Step 1: Master the frog stand - lean forward with knees on elbows (2-4 weeks)
  • Step 2: Practice planche leans against a wall to build shoulder strength (3-4 weeks)
  • Step 3: Work on tuck planche - body tucked in ball position (4-6 weeks)
  • Step 4: Progress to advanced tuck planche - knees closer to chest (4-6 weeks)
  • Step 5: Move to straddle planche - legs spread apart (6-8 weeks)
  • Step 6: Finally achieve full planche - legs together and straight (8-12 weeks)

Supplementary Exercises

  • Pseudo Planche Push-ups: 3 x 5-8 reps - Builds pushing strength with forward lean
  • Plank Shoulder Taps: 3 x 10 - Improves core stability
  • Wrist Circles: 3 x 20 - Builds wrist mobility and strength
  • Compression Work: 3 x 30-60 seconds - Hold tuck position with bent arms
  • Scapular Push-ups: 3 x 10-15 - Develops shoulder blade strength

Handstand

A fundamental skill in calisthenics where you balance upside down on your hands.

How to Achieve Handstand

  • Step 1: Build wrist and shoulder strength with plank holds
  • Step 2: Practice wall handstands with chest to wall
  • Step 3: Hold wall handstands for 60 seconds
  • Step 4: Practice handstand kick-ups away from the wall
  • Step 5: Work on balance by holding for a few seconds
  • Step 6: Increase freestanding handstand time gradually

Muscle-Up

A dynamic movement combining a pull-up with a dip, transitioning from below the bar to above it.

Prerequisites

  • 15+ pull-ups
  • 20+ dips
  • Explosive pulling power

Progression Steps

Level Exercise Reps
1 High Pull-Ups (chest to bar) 3 x 8
2 Jumping Muscle-Ups 3 x 5
3 Negative Muscle-Ups 3 x 5
4 Full Muscle-Up 3 x 3

Front Lever

Hold your body horizontal while hanging from a bar, facing up.

Progression Path

  • Step 1: Tuck front lever - knees tucked to chest (hold 10-15s)
  • Step 2: Advanced tuck - one leg extended slightly (hold 8-12s)
  • Step 3: Straddle front lever - legs spread wide (hold 5-10s)
  • Step 4: One leg front lever - one leg straight (hold 5-8s)
  • Step 5: Full front lever - both legs straight (hold 5+ seconds)
Advanced

Human Flag

Hold your body horizontally sideways while gripping a vertical pole.

How to Train

  • Step 1: Build side plank strength (60+ seconds)
  • Step 2: Practice flag holds with bent knees
  • Step 3: Work on straddle flag - legs apart
  • Step 4: Progress to full flag with legs together

Pull-Ups

The foundational pulling movement that builds back, bicep, and grip strength. Master pull-ups to unlock advanced pulling skills like muscle-ups and front levers.

Progression Steps

  • Step 1: Dead Hang - Hold the bar for 30-60 seconds (2-3 weeks)
  • Step 2: Scapular Pull-Ups - Pull shoulder blades down (2-3 weeks)
  • Step 3: Negative Pull-Ups - Jump up and lower slowly (3-4 weeks)
  • Step 4: Assisted Pull-Ups - Use bands or machine (2-4 weeks)
  • Step 5: First Full Pull-Up - Lower the assistance gradually
  • Step 6: Build Volume - Increase reps to 8-12 per set

Training Variations

  • Wide Grip: Emphasizes lat activation
  • Narrow Grip: Increases bicep engagement
  • Neutral Grip: Balanced muscle activation
  • Archer Pull-Ups: Builds lateral strength (advanced)
  • Weighted Pull-Ups: Add weight belt for strength (advanced)

Common Issues & Fixes

  • Can't do any pull-ups? Start with dead hangs and scapular pulls
  • Stalled at 3-5 reps? Do more volume with negative reps
  • Elbows hurt? Check your form and avoid hyperextension
  • Grip failing first? Train grip strength separately with farmer carries

Bar Dips

The king of upper body pressing movements. Dips build chest, shoulders, and triceps explosively while improving your pressing foundation.

Dips vs Push-Ups

  • Range of Motion: Dips allow deeper chest stretch than push-ups
  • Intensity: Dips are typically harder, requiring more strength
  • Joint Stress: Requires good shoulder mobility to avoid injury
  • Progressions: Can be made easier with assistance bands
  • Variations: Chest-focused or tricep-focused based on lean angle

Progression Path

  • Step 1: Bench Dips - Using a bench or chair (1-2 weeks)
  • Step 2: Assisted Bar Dips - Using resistance bands (2-3 weeks)
  • Step 3: Negative Dips - Jump up and lower slowly (3-4 weeks)
  • Step 4: First Full Dip - One unassisted repetition
  • Step 5: Build to 3-5 reps - Good foundation level
  • Step 6: Add Weight - Use a dip belt for progressive overload

Bridge Hold & Variations

The bridge is an excellent posterior chain builder that strengthens glutes, hamstrings, and lower back without equipment. Perfect for building a foundation for advanced movements.

Benefits

  • Strengthens glutes and hamstrings
  • Improves lower back strength and health
  • Corrects anterior pelvic tilt from sitting
  • No equipment needed
  • Low injury risk for beginners
  • Foundation for advanced leg control

Bridge Progression Levels

  • Level 1: Glute Bridge - 30-60 seconds, 3 sets
  • Level 2: Single Leg Bridge - 30 seconds each leg, 3 sets
  • Level 3: Elevated Single Leg Bridge - One leg on bench/couch
  • Level 4: Weighted Bridge - Barbell or dumbbells across hips
  • Level 5: Bridge Hold with Resistance Bands - Add band resistance
  • Level 6: Shrimp Squat to Bridge - Advanced combination

Bridge Variations

  • Marching Bridge: Alternate lifting legs while holding position
  • Bridge with Glute Squeeze: 1 second squeeze at top of each rep
  • Slider Bridge: Slide feet in and out while bridging
  • Band Pull Bridge: Place band above knees, push outward
  • Stability Ball Bridge: Feet on rolling ball for increased difficulty

Training Tips

  • Focus on glute activation, not lower back arch
  • Squeeze glutes hard at the top of each rep
  • Keep core tight to protect lower back
  • Breathe steadily throughout the hold
  • Increase hold time before adding weight
