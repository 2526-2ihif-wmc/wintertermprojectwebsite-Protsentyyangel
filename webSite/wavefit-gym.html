Getting Started at the Gym

Weight training builds muscle, increases strength, and boosts metabolism.

Basic Rules

  • Progressive Overload: Gradually increase weight over time
  • Proper Form: Quality over quantity
  • Rest: Muscles grow during rest, not workouts
  • Consistency: Regular training is key
  • Eat Protein: 1.6-2g per kg of bodyweight daily

Safety Tips

  • Always Warm Up: 5-10 minutes cardio and stretching
  • Use a Spotter: For heavy lifts
  • Start Light: Learn form before adding weight
  • Listen to Pain: Stop if you feel sharp pain
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink water throughout
  • Gym Etiquette: Wipe equipment, re-rack weights, share equipment

Building Muscle (Hypertrophy)

Hypertrophy training focuses on muscle growth through metabolic stress, mechanical tension, and muscle damage. This is ideal for aesthetic goals and building size.

Hypertrophy Training Principles

  • Rep Range: 8-12 reps per set is optimal for muscle growth
  • Time Under Tension: 40-60 seconds per set maximizes growth
  • Volume: 10-20 sets per muscle group per week
  • Frequency: Hit each muscle 2-3x per week
  • Rest Periods: 60-90 seconds between sets
  • Progressive Overload: Gradually increase weight or reps

Sample Hypertrophy Workout Split (4 Days)

  • Day 1 - Chest/Triceps: Bench, Incline Bench, Flyes, Tricep Dips, Overhead Extension
  • Day 2 - Back/Biceps: Rows, Lat Pulldowns, Deadlifts, Barbell Curls, Face Pulls
  • Day 3 - Rest or Light Cardio
  • Day 4 - Legs: Squats, Leg Press, Leg Curls, Leg Extensions, Calf Raises
  • Day 5 - Shoulders: Overhead Press, Lateral Raises, Rear Delt Flyes, Shrugs

Nutrition for Muscle Growth

  • Protein: 1.6-2.2g per kg of body weight daily
  • Carbs: 4-7g per kg for energy and recovery
  • Fats: 0.5-1.5g per kg for hormone production
  • Calorie Surplus: 300-500 calories above maintenance
  • Meal Timing: Eat protein and carbs post-workout

Advanced: Push/Pull/Legs Split

The PPL split is one of the most effective training structures for intermediate to advanced lifters, allowing high frequency and volume for muscle growth and strength.

Push Day Exercises

  • Horizontal Pushing: Bench Press (3-4 sets x 6-12 reps)
  • Incline Pushing: Incline Bench (3 sets x 8-10 reps)
  • Vertical Pushing: Overhead Press (3 sets x 6-10 reps)
  • Chest Isolation: Cable Flyes (3 sets x 12-15 reps)
  • Tricep Work: Dips or Skull Crushers (3 sets x 8-12 reps)
  • Lateral Raises: Shoulder work (3 sets x 10-15 reps)

Pull Day Exercises

  • Horizontal Pulling: Barbell Rows (4 sets x 6-8 reps)
  • Vertical Pulling: Pull-Ups or Lat Pulldowns (3-4 sets x 6-12 reps)
  • Secondary Row: Dumbbell Rows (3 sets x 8-12 reps)
  • Back Isolation: Face Pulls (3 sets x 12-15 reps)
  • Bicep Work: Barbell Curls (3 sets x 8-12 reps)
  • Bicep Isolation: Hammer Curls (3 sets x 10-12 reps)

Leg Day Exercises

  • Primary Squat: Barbell Squats (4 sets x 6-8 reps)
  • Secondary Squat: Front Squats or Leg Press (3 sets x 8-10 reps)
  • Hamstring Focus: Romanian Deadlifts (3 sets x 6-10 reps)
  • Quad Isolation: Leg Extensions (3 sets x 12-15 reps)
  • Hamstring Isolation: Leg Curls (3 sets x 10-12 reps)
  • Calf Work: Calf Raises (3 sets x 15-20 reps)

PPL Split Benefits

  • High training frequency (6 days/week) for muscle groups
  • Balanced muscle development
  • High volume for strength and hypertrophy
  • Allows recovery between similar movement patterns
  • Great for advanced lifters
