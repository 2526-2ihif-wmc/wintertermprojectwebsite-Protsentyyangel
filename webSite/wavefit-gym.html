Getting Started at the Gym

Weight training builds muscle, increases strength, and boosts metabolism.

Basic Rules

  • Progressive Overload: Gradually increase weight over time
  • Proper Form: Quality over quantity
  • Rest: Muscles grow during rest, not workouts
  • Consistency: Regular training is key
  • Eat Protein: 1.6-2g per kg of bodyweight daily

Intermediate: Push/Pull/Legs Split

6 days per week for intermediate lifters

Day 1 & 4: Push (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)

  • Bench Press: 4 sets x 6-8 reps
  • Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Overhead Press: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Lateral Raises: 3 sets x 12-15 reps
  • Tricep Dips: 3 sets x 8-12 reps

Day 2 & 5: Pull (Back, Biceps)

  • Deadlifts: 4 sets x 5-6 reps
  • Pull-ups: 3 sets x 6-10 reps
  • Barbell Rows: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Face Pulls: 3 sets x 12-15 reps
  • Barbell Curls: 3 sets x 8-12 reps

Day 3 & 6: Legs

  • Squats: 4 sets x 6-8 reps
  • Romanian Deadlifts: 3 sets x 8-10 reps
  • Leg Press: 3 sets x 10-12 reps
  • Leg Curls: 3 sets x 10-12 reps
  • Calf Raises: 4 sets x 15-20 reps

Day 7: Rest

Safety Tips

  • Always Warm Up: 5-10 minutes cardio and stretching
  • Use a Spotter: For heavy lifts
  • Start Light: Learn form before adding weight
  • Listen to Pain: Stop if you feel sharp pain
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink water throughout
  • Gym Etiquette: Wipe equipment, re-rack weights, share equipment
